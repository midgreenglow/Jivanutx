Understanding the Gut Ecosystem

The human gut hosts trillions of microbes that play a central role in digestion, immune regulation, and metabolic health. When this ecosystem becomes imbalanced-a condition known as gut dysbiosis-it has been linked to chronic gastrointestinal and inflammatory conditions.

Fecal Microbiota Transplantation (FMT) is a microbiome-based approach that aims to restore this balance by introducing healthy, carefully screened gut microbes into the recipient. Over the last decade, FMT has emerged as one of the most promising microbiome interventions worldwide.
