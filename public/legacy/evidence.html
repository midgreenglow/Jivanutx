Clinical Evidence: FMT in Gut-Related Diseases

Providing high transparency and science-driven insights into the therapeutic potential of the microbiome.

Overview

Fecal Microbiota Transplantation (FMT) is a microbiome-based approach designed to restore healthy gut microbial balance. It has been examined across multiple clinical settings, showing the strongest evidence in inflammatory and infectious gut diseases.

1. Ulcerative Colitis (UC)

Real-World Evidence (India)

A prospective real-world analysis conducted at Dayanand Medical College & Hospital (Ludhiana) involving steroid-dependent UC patients treated with repeated FMT sessions via colonoscopy demonstrated significant outcomes:

  • ~46% achieved steroid-free clinical remission.
  • ~76% showed clinical response; ~63% achieved endoscopic remission at 24 weeks.
  • Responders successfully withdrew from steroids without serious adverse effects [1].

Multicenter Randomized Controlled Trial (India)

An open-label RCT involving leading institutions (AIIMS Delhi, PGIMER, Lisie, IMS BHU, Lokmanya Tilak, and Dayanand) evaluated multi-donor FMT combined with an anti-inflammatory diet. The study showed effective induction and maintenance of remission over one year when combined with standard care [2].

Global Evidence

Meta-analyses of multiple Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) confirm that FMT significantly increases clinical and endoscopic remission rates compared to control groups, validating its therapeutic potential globally [3].

Summary: Both controlled and real-world data support FMT’s role in inducing remission in UC, with substantial clinical evidence contributed by major Indian medical centers.

2. Recurrent Clostridioides difficile Infection (CDI)

Tertiary Care Insights (India)

In high-risk cases, such as hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HSCT) recipients with refractory or recurrent CDI, FMT administered at a tertiary center in India led to a complete resolution of infection in the majority of patients with minimal unrelated adverse events [4].

Global Context

Systematic reviews consistently show that FMT provides a massive increase in resolution rates for recurrent CDI compared to standard antibiotic therapy, maintaining high cure rates across international studies [5].

Summary: FMT is highly effective in resolving recurrent CDI, even in immunocompromised patients, aligning Indian clinical success with global benchmarks.

3. Parkinson’s Disease (Gut–Brain Axis)

Emerging research into the gut–brain axis has led to clinical trials exploring FMT for Parkinson’s Disease. Early pilot studies suggest that FMT is well-tolerated and can successfully shift gut microbial profiles, with some patients reporting improvements in gastrointestinal symptoms like constipation. However, evidence for direct neurologic benefit remains exploratory [6].

4. Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

International Research

FMT in ASD is being actively explored globally. Clinical trials assess safety and microbiome changes in children, with some prospective studies showing improvements in both GI symptoms and core behavioral symptoms without serious adverse events [7].

Emerging Research in India

The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) has approved FMT research for autistic children at the Resplice Autism Institute in Hyderabad. This reflects the growing clinical interest in microbiome influences on neurodevelopmental conditions in India [8].
