Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 03 February 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Jivanu Therapeutics Pvt. Ltd. (“Jivanu,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you access our website, purchase products, submit biological samples, participate in research or clinical programs, or otherwise use our services (collectively, the “Services”).

By using the Services, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy and any applicable addenda or informed consent documents.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect personal identifiers and contact details, account credentials, billing information, and healthcare provider or referral details where relevant to the Services.

2.2 Health and Biological Information

Depending on the nature of the Services, we may collect biological samples (including stool samples), microbiome sequencing data (such as 16S rRNA data), clinical metadata, lifestyle information, treatment response data, and follow-up information. Such information may constitute sensitive personal data under applicable law.

2.3 Technical Information

We may automatically collect technical information such as IP address, browser type, device identifiers, and website usage analytics to support functionality, security, and performance monitoring.

3. Purpose of Use

Information collected is used to provide and manage Services, perform microbiome analysis and reporting, support clinical and investigational activities, conduct research and development, comply with regulatory obligations, and communicate service-related updates.

4. Consent and Withdrawal

By submitting personal or biological information, placing an order, or enrolling in a study, you provide express consent for collection and use as described in this Policy.

You may withdraw consent by contacting us via the Contact Us page, subject to legal, regulatory, contractual, and safety obligations. Withdrawal of consent may limit access to certain Services.

5. Research and De-Identified Data

We may use de-identified or anonymized data derived from Services for research, product validation, regulatory submissions, and scientific publication. Once de-identified, such data is no longer considered personal information under applicable law.

6. Disclosure of Information

Information may be disclosed to certified laboratories, healthcare professionals, research collaborators, service providers, and regulatory authorities as required to deliver Services or comply with law. We do not sell personal or health data.

7. Data Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information. However, no system can guarantee absolute security.

8. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request access to, correction of, deletion of, or restriction on processing of your personal information, or withdraw consent, by contacting us.

9. Age of Consent

Services are intended for individuals of legal age. Participation by minors requires appropriate parental or guardian consent and medical oversight.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Updates will be posted on our website and take effect upon publication.

ADDENDUM A: Clinical Trial & Investigational Services Privacy

This Addendum applies to individuals participating in clinical trials, IND studies, or other investigational services. Personal and health data may be collected pursuant to approved clinical protocols, informed consent documents, ethics committee approvals, and applicable regulations.

Required data collection may include medical history, eligibility data, adverse event reporting, and longitudinal follow-up information. Such data may be retained as required by law for safety, audit, and regulatory compliance, even if consent is withdrawn.

ADDENDUM B: Biological Sample Retention & Biobanking

Biological samples may include stool samples, derived microbial material, isolates, or related biological material. Samples may be retained for service completion, quality control, regulatory compliance, and approved research activities.

Where feasible, samples are coded or de-identified to reduce re-identification risk. Retention does not imply transfer of ownership or commercial rights beyond what is expressly consented.

For privacy-related questions, please contact us via the Contact Us page.
