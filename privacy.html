Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 03-02-2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Jivanu Therapeutics Pvt. Ltd. (“Company,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you access our website, purchase products, submit biological samples, participate in research or clinical trials, or otherwise use our services (collectively, the “Services”).

By using the Services, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy and any applicable addenda.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect:

  • Name, address, email address, phone number
  • Account and login credentials
  • Billing and payment information
  • Healthcare provider or referral information

2.2 Health & Biological Information

Depending on the Services, we may collect:

  • Stool samples and derived biological material
  • Microbiome sequencing data (including 16S rRNA data)
  • Clinical, lifestyle, and health metadata
  • Treatment response and follow-up information

This information may constitute Sensitive Personal Data or Protected Health Information under applicable law.

2.3 Technical Information

We automatically collect:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Device identifiers
  • Website usage and analytics data

3. Purpose of Use

We collect and use information to:

  • Provide, manage, and improve Services
  • Perform microbiome analysis and reporting
  • Deliver clinical, investigational, or therapeutic services
  • Conduct scientific research and development
  • Support regulatory, safety, and compliance obligations
  • Communicate operational and service-related updates

4. Consent

By submitting personal or biological information, placing an order, or enrolling in a study, you provide express consent for collection and use as described herein.

Where required, additional consent will be obtained for secondary purposes such as marketing.

Withdrawal of Consent

You may withdraw consent by contacting Contact Us, subject to regulatory, contractual, and safety obligations. Withdrawal may limit access to certain Services.

5. Research & De-Identified Data

We may use de-identified or anonymized data derived from Services for:

  • Research and development
  • Product validation and improvement
  • Regulatory submissions
  • Scientific publication

Once de-identified, such data is no longer considered personal information under applicable law.

6. Disclosure of Information

We may disclose information to:

  • Certified laboratories and sequencing providers
  • Healthcare professionals involved in your care
  • Research collaborators (using de-identified data unless otherwise consented)
  • Service providers supporting operations (e.g., cloud hosting, logistics)
  • Government or regulatory authorities where required by law

We do not sell personal or health data.

7. E-Commerce & Payment Processing

  • Our online store may be hosted on third-party platforms
  • Payment information is encrypted and processed in accordance with PCI-DSS standards
  • Transaction data is retained only as long as necessary to complete the transaction

8. Cookies

We use cookies to support functionality, security, and analytics. You may disable cookies through browser settings; some features may be limited.

9. Data Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards, including encryption, access controls, and secure laboratory and data environments. No system can guarantee absolute security.

10. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access or correct personal information
  • Request deletion or restriction of processing
  • Withdraw consent
  • Lodge a complaint with a regulatory authority

Requests may be submitted to Contact Us.

11. Age of Consent

Services are intended for individuals of legal age. Participation by minors requires appropriate parental or guardian consent and medical oversight.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Material changes will be posted on our website and take effect upon publication.

ADDENDUM A: CLINICAL TRIAL & INVESTIGATIONAL SERVICES PRIVACY

This Addendum applies to individuals participating in clinical trials, IND studies, or other investigational services.

A.1 Regulatory Basis

Personal and health information may be collected and processed pursuant to:

  • Approved clinical protocols
  • Informed consent documents
  • Ethics committee / IRB approvals
  • Applicable drug and medical research regulations

A.2 Required Data Collection

Participation may require collection of:

  • Medical history and eligibility data
  • Adverse event and safety data
  • Longitudinal follow-up information

Such data collection is mandatory for trial participation and regulatory compliance.

A.3 Use & Disclosure

Clinical trial data may be shared with:

  • Investigators and study staff
  • Ethics committees / IRBs
  • Regulatory authorities (e.g., DCGI, FDA, EMA)
  • Contract research organizations (CROs)

Data will be coded or de-identified where possible.

A.4 Retention

Clinical trial data will be retained for periods required by applicable law and regulatory guidance, even if consent is withdrawn, where necessary for safety, audit, or compliance purposes.

ADDENDUM B: BIOLOGICAL SAMPLE RETENTION & BIOBANKING POLICY

B.1 Sample Types

Samples may include stool, derived microbial material, isolates, or related biological material.

B.2 Purpose of Retention

Samples may be retained for:

  • Completion of requested Services
  • Quality control and validation
  • Regulatory compliance
  • Approved research and development

B.3 Retention Period

Unless otherwise required by law or consent:

  • Diagnostic samples are retained for a limited period
  • Clinical trial samples are retained per protocol and regulatory requirements
  • Research samples may be stored long-term in a controlled biobank

B.4 De-Identification

Where feasible, retained samples are coded or de-identified to reduce re-identification risk.

B.5 Destruction Requests

You may request destruction of remaining samples by contacting Contact Us, subject to:

  • Legal and regulatory obligations
  • Active clinical or safety requirements
  • Completed data integrity processes

B.6 No Ownership Transfer

Retention does not imply transfer of ownership or commercial rights to the donor beyond what is expressly consented.
